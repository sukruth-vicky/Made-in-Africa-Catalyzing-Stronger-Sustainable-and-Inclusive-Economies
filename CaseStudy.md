Manufacturing plays a critical role in driving economic growth, job creation, and long-term resilience in emerging economies. In this case, McKinsey & Company partnered with the UK’s Foreign, Commonwealth & Development Office (FCDO) to implement the Manufacturing Africa program, designed to accelerate industrial development across the continent.

Africa is experiencing rapid population growth, urbanization, and a rising demand for jobs. With the population expected to double by 2050 and a large share under the age of 15, the need for sustainable employment opportunities is urgent. Manufacturing was identified as a key lever to drive inclusive economic growth and reduce dependency on imports.

To address these challenges, McKinsey supported the creation of a platform that connects investors, governments, and manufacturers. Instead of directly funding projects, the program focuses on catalyzing foreign direct investment (FDI) by providing technical assistance, financial modeling, and strategic advisory support to viable manufacturing opportunities.

The initiative operates across multiple African countries including Ethiopia, Kenya, Nigeria, Senegal, and Rwanda. It supports sectors such as agro-processing, textiles, packaging, consumer electronics, and green manufacturing. By strengthening local institutions and investment pipelines, the program helps create sustainable industrial ecosystems.

McKinsey’s role includes conducting sector analyses, supporting deal structuring, and helping governments and investment agencies attract capital efficiently. The approach emphasizes collaboration between public and private stakeholders to ensure long-term scalability and impact.

The program has already delivered measurable results:

• Over $850 million in foreign direct investment secured
• More than 16,000 jobs created across supported projects
• 28+ manufacturing deals closed
• A $2 billion investment pipeline expected to generate at least 30,000 additional jobs

This case highlights how strategic investment, cross-sector collaboration, and data-driven advisory can unlock industrial growth and create inclusive economic opportunities across emerging markets.
